4-H

Head, Heart, Hands, Health
A national youth development organization administered by USDA through land-grant universities. Members aged 5‚Äì19 complete hands-on projects in science, agriculture, citizenship, and healthy living.

4-H is one of the largest youth development programs in the United States, reaching nearly six million young people annually. The four H's represent the organization's pledge: "I pledge my Head to clearer thinking, my Heart to greater loyalty, my Hands to larger service, and my Health to better living."

At state and county fairs, 4-H members exhibit projects they have worked on throughout the year. These projects span a remarkable range ‚Äî from raising livestock and growing crops to robotics, photography, public speaking, and fashion design. Each project is evaluated by judges using published scoring rubrics, and participants receive feedback along with ribbon awards.

The organization is structured at three levels: national (administered by USDA's National Institute of Food and Agriculture), state (managed by each land-grant university's Cooperative Extension Service), and local (run by volunteer leaders in community clubs).

"My daughter has been in 4-H since she was a Cloverbud, and this year she's showing her market lamb at the State Fair for the first time."
